01

Film · Mental Health Awareness

"Inversion" — A short film on postpartum depression

A scripted short film created to raise awareness about postpartum depression in Nigerian communities, developed from research into women's lived experiences and the cultural silence surrounding maternal mental health.

Context

Postpartum depression is often unspoken in many Nigerian communities, where motherhood is expected to be joyful and emotionally uncomplicated. I approached this silence as both a storytelling gap and a communication challenge.

My Role

Researcher and scriptwriter. I developed the narrative from initial research through to final script production, collaborating with a media team to bring the film to completion.

Research & Development

I explored women's experiences of postpartum depression through secondary research and qualitative listening, focusing on how cultural expectations shape whether women speak about emotional distress after childbirth.

Approach

Rather than presenting information directly, I chose fiction as the primary medium. The film format allowed the subject to be experienced emotionally rather than explained clinically. The title Inversion reflects the contrast between societal expectations of motherhood and private emotional reality.

Key Insight

This project reinforced the role of storytelling in shaping how health issues are understood. In this context, communication is not only about information — it is about creating space for experiences that are often left unspoken.

02

Field Research · Advocacy Communication

Advocacy on Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) in Akwuke, Enugu State

Volunteer research and communication work for the Society for the Improvement of Rural People, funded by the Wallace Global Fund through the Global Media Campaign to End FGM.

Context

The project aimed to end Female Genital Mutilation in Akwuke, Enugu State — a deeply entrenched cultural practice — through community engagement, behavior change communication, and survivor-centered advocacy. I joined as a volunteer researcher, contributing to the research and documentation phases.

My Role

Volunteer researcher and writer. I conducted semi-structured interviews with participants, transcribed and coded qualitative data, and synthesized findings into a summary report used to guide project activities. I also authored a published Medium article documenting field experiences and survivor stories.

Research Process

Using semi-structured interview protocols, I gathered first-person accounts from community members about barriers to FGM abandonment and the drivers of behavior change. I applied thematic analysis to surface patterns across responses — identifying recurring tensions between community pressure, economic incentives, and the desire to protect daughters.

Communication Approach

The published article was written for a broader public audience beyond the project itself. I focused on centering survivor voices and personal transformation rather than statistics alone, believing that emotional resonance drives public engagement with difficult health and rights issues.

Key Insight

This project deepened my understanding of how research findings must be translated differently depending on audience — a funder report, a practitioner brief, and a public essay serve very different communicative purposes, even when they draw from the same data.
